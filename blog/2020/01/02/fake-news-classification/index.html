Fake News Classification with Logistic Regression?

Thu 02 January 2020

Fake news is for from of news that contains deliberate disinformation which is spread using traditional and or online social media. In this blog post, we construct a simple logistic regression model to classify news articles to be fake or real. We'll be using Term Frequency and Inverse Data Frequency to vectorize our data.

I try to recreate and improve the Bayesian model from George McIntire detailed in this post with logistic regression.

Feature Extraction¶

We'll use term frequency to model the text data and make our predictions. We can calculate the term frequency by counting how many times a word appears in a document compared to the total amount of words in the document. This means that each news article in our dataset gets its own Term Frequency.

$$tf_{i,j} = \frac{n_{i,j}}{\sum_kn_{i,j}}$$

We use Inverse Data Frequency (IDF) to calculate the weight of rare words across all documents. The rare words get a high IDF score. More common words get a lower IDF score.

$$idf(w) = \log(\frac{N}{df_t})$$

When combing the TF and IDF we get the TF-IDF score for a word in the document.

$$w_{i,j} = tf_{i,j} \times \log(\frac{N}{df_i})$$

Where $tf_{i,j}$ is the number of occurrences of $i$ in $j$. $df_i$ the number of documents containing $i$, and $N$ is the total number of documents.

We use TF-IDF words to create a sparse matrix for the words in the articles.

In [11]:
vectorizer_word = TfidfVectorizer(strip_accents="unicode", analyzer="word", stop_words="english", ngram_range=(1,1), max_features=1000)

vector_train_word = vectorizer_word.fit_transform(X_train)
vector_valid_word = vectorizer_word.transform(X_valid)

Furthermore, we also use TF-IDF to create a sparse matrix for the characters in the articles.

In [ ]:
vectorizer_char = TfidfVectorizer(sublinear_tf=True, strip_accents='unicode',analyzer='char',stop_words='english', ngram_range=(2, 6),max_features=50000)

vector_train_char = vectorizer_char.fit_transform(X_train)
vector_valid_char = vectorizer_char.transform(X_valid)

We use the hstack() function from SciPy to stack the sparse word and character matrices.

In [ ]:
train_features = hstack([vector_train_word, vector_train_char])
validation_features = hstack([vector_valid_word, vector_valid_char])

Modeling the Data¶

In [ ]:
logreg = LogisticRegression()
logreg.fit(train_features, y_train)
pred = logreg.predict(validation_features)
f"Accuracy: {accuracy_score(y_valid, pred) * 100 :.2f}%"

We get an accuracy score of around 95% on the validation set!
